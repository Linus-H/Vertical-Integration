For differential equations the identity for derivatives is key:
\begin{align*}
\mathcal{L} \left\{ \frac{dx}{dt}(t) \right\} &=\int_0^\infty \frac{dx}{dt}(t)\exp (-st)dt\\
&=x(t)\exp(-st)\rvert _0^\infty + s  \int_0^\infty x(t)\exp (-st)dt\\
&=-x(t=0) + sX(s)
\end{align*}
